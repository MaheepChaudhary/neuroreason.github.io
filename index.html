The Problem: Why AI Transparency Matters

As AI systems become more complex, understanding their decision-making process is increasingly critical.

Modern AI systems, particularly large language models and neural networks, operate as "black boxes" - making decisions without revealing their reasoning process. This lack of transparency creates significant challenges:

  • Regulatory compliance issues in sectors like finance and healthcare
  • Inability to identify and correct biases and errors in AI reasoning
  • Reduced trust from users and stakeholders
  • Difficulty in debugging and improving AI systems

For enterprises deploying AI solutions, this opacity creates significant business and compliance risks, hampering adoption in high-stakes environments.

Causal Abstraction: A Theoretical Foundation for Mechanistic Interpretability

Geiger, A., Ibeling, D., Zur, A., Chaudhary, M., Chauhan, S., Huang, J., Arora, A., Wu, Z., Goodman, N., Potts, C., Icard, T.

2025

This foundational research provides the theoretical framework for mechanistic interpretability, generalizing causal abstraction theory to include arbitrary mechanism transformation. It formalizes core concepts like modular features and graded faithfulness while unifying various interpretability methodologies under causal abstraction.

Towards Trustworthy and Aligned Machine Learning: A Data-centric Survey with Causality Perspectives

Liu, H., Chaudhary, M., Wang, H.

2024

This survey provides a systematic overview of trustworthy machine learning advancements, focusing on robustness, adversarial resilience, interpretability, and fairness from a data-centric perspective. It establishes a unified framework using Pearl's hierarchy of causality to categorize techniques across these domains.

Evaluating open-source sparse autoencoders on disentangling factual knowledge in gpt-2 small

Chaudhary, M., Geiger, A.

2024

This research evaluates whether sparse autoencoders (SAEs) trained on hidden representations of GPT-2 small have features that separately mediate different types of knowledge. The study compares four open-source SAEs against neuron baselines and distributed alignment search methods, providing valuable insights for knowledge disentanglement.

Modular Training of Neural Networks aids Interpretability

Golechha, S., Chaudhary, M., Velja, J., Abate, A., Schoots, N.

2025

This paper proposes using clusterability to improve neural network interpretability. The researchers develop a "clusterability loss" function that encourages the formation of non-interacting clusters within neural networks, leading to more modular models with disjoint, smaller circuits that are easier to interpret and understand.
